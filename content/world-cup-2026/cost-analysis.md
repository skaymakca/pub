### The Bottom Line

**Scenario B saves approximately $4,800** in family costs compared to Scenario A.

Breakdown of the savings:
- **~$902 less in flights** (2 fewer BOS→DFW at $255, 2 fewer DFW→YYZ at $327, offset by 2x BOS→YTZ Porter at $131)
- ~$300–500 less in meals/activities (fewer Dallas days for S+M)
- **~$3,100–4,000 in ticket revenue** (the big difference)

The ticket revenue remains the swing factor. The actual travel cost difference between A and B is only ~$1,200. It's the $3,100+ from selling Dallas tickets that makes Scenario B significantly cheaper.

**FIL/BIL costs (~$2,900) are fully separate** — they don't affect the family budget comparison.

**Note:** Toronto hotel costs are the largest single line item (~$2,553–3,374 USD for 6 nights). Residence Inn is already SOLD OUT — book alternatives ASAP. See [10 — Hotel Pricing](../hotel-pricing/) for actual rates.

## United MileagePlus Strategy

With **34,882 miles** available, the best uses are:

| Use | Miles Needed | Cash Saved | Value (cpp) | Verdict |
|-----|-------------|------------|-------------|---------|
| BOS→DFW (1 pax, after 5pm) | 26.4k + $5.60 | $249–311 | 0.95–1.18 | Decent — especially for the after-5pm option |
| BOS→PHL (1 pax, JetBlue nonstop) | 15k + $5.60 | $78–113 | 0.52–0.76 | OK but cash is cheap |
| PHL→BOS (1 pax, JetBlue nonstop) | 15k + $5.60 | $78 | 0.52 | Better to pay cash |
| LAX→PHL (FIL, 1 pax) | 15k + $5.60 | $204–224 | 1.36–1.49 | Best value if FIL has UA miles |

**Recommended:** Use 26.4k miles on one BOS→DFW ticket (saves ~$311 on the AA nonstop after-5pm fare). Keep remaining 8.5k miles for future use. The BOS↔PHL JetBlue flights are cheap enough in cash that miles aren't worth it.

**Key discovery:** JetBlue nonstops BOS↔PHL are bookable as United MileagePlus Saver Awards at 15k miles. Prior research incorrectly concluded JetBlue doesn't serve BOS→PHL — they do, and they're bookable through United.
