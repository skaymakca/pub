This is the one open question in the World Cup travel plan. Marina + Luka are going to Dallas regardless. The question is whether Sukru + Maja tag along for the full Dallas -> Toronto -> (split) journey, or skip Dallas and fly directly to Toronto to meet up.

---

## The Two Scenarios

### Scenario A: Full Family -- Dallas + Toronto

**Trip length for Sukru + Maja:** 9 nights (Jun 15-24)

```
Jun 15 (Mon)  BOS -> DFW (all 4)
Jun 16 (Tue)  Dallas sightseeing
Jun 17 (Wed)  GAME -- England vs Croatia, 3 PM CT
Jun 18 (Thu)  DFW -> YYZ (all 4)
Jun 19-22     Toronto sightseeing
Jun 23 (Tue)  GAME -- Panama vs Croatia, 7 PM ET
Jun 24 (Wed)  YYZ -> BOS (Sukru + Maja go home)
```

### Scenario B (Updated): Sukru + Maja Skip Dallas, All 4 Fly to Toronto Together

**Trip length for Sukru + Maja:** 4 nights (Jun 20-24) or 5 nights (Jun 19-24)

```
Jun 15 (Mon)  Marina + Luka: BOS -> DFW
Jun 16-17     Dallas sightseeing + GAME (Marina + Luka)
Jun 18 (Thu)  Marina + Luka: DFW -> BOS (return home)
Jun 19 (Fri)  Option: All 4 BOS -> YYZ (5 nights -- M+L have only ~18h recovery)
Jun 20 (Sat)  Option: All 4 BOS -> YYZ (4 nights -- full recovery day for M+L)
Jun 21-22     Toronto sightseeing
Jun 23 (Tue)  GAME -- Panama vs Croatia, 7 PM ET
Jun 24 (Wed)  All 4: Toronto -> BOS (home)
```

Marina + Luka do Dallas on their own (Jun 15-18), fly home, then the whole family goes to Toronto together.

## What Dallas Offers Sukru + Maja

### For Maja (age 7)

- **Six Flags Hurricane Harbor** -- 47-acre water park, Splash Island family area, lazy river, wave pool. Best way to beat 95°F heat. This is Maja's speed.
- **Universal Kids Resort** (Frisco, if open by mid-June) -- Gabby's Dollhouse, Trolls, SpongeBob, Despicable Me. Purpose-built for her age group. Would be a highlight of the trip.
- **Dallas Zoo** -- 106 acres, 2,000+ animals. Good for a half-day.
- **Perot Museum "Soccer: More Than a Game"** -- interactive soccer science exhibit. Kick balls, design jerseys, build Lego stadiums.
- **Klyde Warren Park** -- free, 35-ft climbing tower, splash features, food trucks.

### For Sukru

- **BBQ pilgrimage** -- Pecan Lodge (globally ranked by Texas Monthly), Lockhart Smokehouse, Smoky Rose
- **The game itself** -- England vs Croatia at 80,000-seat AT&T Stadium. This is a marquee World Cup match. The 2018 semifinal rematch. The atmosphere will be electric.
- **FIFA Fan Festival at Fair Park** -- free, 35,000 capacity, 1M sq ft

### The Match: England vs Croatia

This is the biggest game of the three. England's massive fanbase, the 2018 semifinal rematch narrative, 80k stadium, and it's the opening group game. Dallas will be buzzing. Missing this to save money is a legitimate regret risk.

## What Toronto Offers Sukru + Maja

### For Maja (age 7)

- **Toronto Islands / Centreville** -- ferry ride + 30 amusement park rides + beaches. A magical half-day.
- **Ripley's Aquarium** -- underwater tunnel, touch tanks, stingrays. Right downtown.
- **CN Tower** -- glass floor at 346m. Thrilling for a 7-year-old.
- **Canada's Wonderland** -- 200+ attractions, 18 coasters, 20-acre water park. KidZville and Planet Snoopy have 25+ rides for younger kids.
- **Royal Ontario Museum** -- WonderWorks gallery is hands-on play-learning for younger kids.

### For Sukru

- **St. Lawrence Market** -- one of the world's best food markets (National Geographic top 10)
- **Kensington Market** -- bohemian neighborhood, diverse food, vintage shops
- **Little Italy / College Street** -- great pre-game dining
- **The game itself** -- Panama vs Croatia at intimate 30k BMO Field. Toronto has a big Croatian community, so the atmosphere will feel like a home game.

### Bonus: Toronto is Lower-Key

- Weather: 75°F vs Dallas's 95°F. Huge difference with a 7-year-old.
- Walkable: no car rental needed. TTC streetcar + walking covers everything.
- Border adventure: crossing into Canada is an experience for kids.
- CAD exchange rate: everything is ~28% cheaper in USD.

## The Arguments

### The "Experience" Argument (Go to Dallas -- Scenario A)

1. **England vs Croatia is a once-in-a-lifetime game.** You can't replicate this. The 2018 semifinal rematch, 80k fans, opening day energy. Sukru and Maja will never get this chance again.

2. **Maja is 7 -- she'll remember this forever.** The spectacle of a World Cup match, the face paint, the songs, the crowd roaring. At 7, she's old enough to absorb it and young enough to be wide-eyed.

3. **Family together for the full adventure.** Two weeks on the road, two countries, two World Cup matches together. This is the kind of family story you tell for decades.

4. **Dallas has great kid stuff.** Hurricane Harbor, Universal Kids Resort (if open), Perot Museum soccer exhibit. It's not just "going for the game."

5. **Luka's named after Luka Modric.** This is a family pilgrimage. Being there for Croatia's opening match -- with Modric potentially playing his last World Cup at age 40 -- is meaningful.

### The "Practical" Argument (Skip Dallas -- Scenario B)

1. **9 nights on the road vs 4-5 nights.** With a 7-year-old, that's a significant difference in energy, meltdowns, laundry, and exhaustion. With the updated Scenario B, S+M are only away for a long weekend.

2. **~$5,000-5,550 total cost difference.** Not just ticket revenue (~$3,500) -- the shorter Toronto stay saves ~$450-900 on hotels and the different flight routing saves ~$1,100. This is enough to fund a separate family vacation.

3. **Dallas is the hardest city of the three.** 95°F+ heat, car rental required, no public transit to the stadium, Arlington is isolated from downtown. With Maja, every outdoor activity requires heat management.

4. **The Toronto trip is plenty.** 6 nights in Toronto, a World Cup match, CN Tower, islands, aquarium -- Maja gets a full vacation without the grind of Dallas.

5. **Marina + Luka get quality time.** A mother-son trip to Dallas has its own magic. Luka gets undivided attention for 3 days.

6. **Sukru + Maja can have their own adventure at home.** Or do a mini-trip somewhere else. Sukru doesn't have to sit at home while Marina is in Dallas.

## The Regret Test

Ask yourself two questions:

**1. "Will I regret NOT being at England vs Croatia?"**

This is the marquee match. 80,000 people. Modric's (likely) last World Cup. The 2018 semifinal rematch. If Croatia wins, it'll be one of those "I was there" moments. If you skip it and watch on TV from Newton, how will that feel?

**2. "Will I regret dragging Maja through 9 days of travel in June heat?"**

If Maja has a meltdown on Day 6 in 95°F Dallas heat and you're thinking "we should have just done Toronto," how will that feel?

## Middle Ground Options

If you're torn, consider:

1. **Fly to Dallas just for the game.** BOS -> DFW on Jun 16, game on Jun 17, DFW -> YYZ on Jun 18. One hotel night in Dallas, skip the sightseeing, minimize the heat exposure. You're there for the match and nothing else.

2. **Sell 2 Dallas tickets now, buy them back later if you change your mind.** List on April 2 at a high price. If they sell, decision made. If not, you still have them.

---

## Bottom Line

There's no wrong answer. Both scenarios are great trips.

- If **the game matters more than comfort** -> Scenario A
- If **comfort and money matter more than the game** -> Scenario B
- If **you want a compromise** -> fly in for just the Dallas game (1 night), then Toronto
