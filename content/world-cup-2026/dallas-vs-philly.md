### Architecture

| | Dallas | Philadelphia |
|---|--------|-------------|
| **Star attraction** | **Dallas Arts District** -- 68 acres, 19 blocks of starchitect buildings. One of the best free architecture walks in any US city. | **City Hall** -- world's largest free-standing masonry building. French Second Empire. Observation deck with panoramic views. |
| **Starchitects represented** | I.M. Pei (Meyerson Symphony Center + City Hall), Renzo Piano (Nasher), Norman Foster (Winspear Opera House), Rem Koolhaas/OMA (Wyly Theatre), Santiago Calatrava (Margaret Hunt Hill Bridge) | Norman Foster (Comcast Technology Center -- tallest building in Philly), Tod Williams Billie Tsien (Barnes Foundation), Frank Furness (PAFA -- High Victorian Gothic masterpiece, reopening April 2026) |
| **Iconic structures** | **Fountain Place** -- 60-story prism with 172 fountains. **Reunion Tower** -- observation deck with 360° views. | **Benjamin Franklin Parkway** -- modeled after the Champs-Élysées (1917). City Beautiful movement. Leads from City Hall to the Art Museum. |
| **Historic** | **Fair Park** -- world's largest collection of Art Deco buildings. **Hotel Adolphus** (1912 Beaux-Arts). | **Old City** -- cobblestone streets, Federal-era buildings, Independence Hall (1753). PAFA Frank Furness building (1876). Centuries of layered history. |
| **Walkability** | Arts District is walkable within itself, but Fair Park / Deep Ellum require driving | **Fully walkable** -- City Hall -> Parkway -> Art Museum is a 2-3 hour architecture stroll |
| **Verdict** | **★★★★★** -- the Arts District starchitect concentration is genuinely world-class | **★★★★★** -- different flavor: deeper historic layers, City Hall is extraordinary, the Parkway is grand. Tie. |

**The split:** Luka (the football fan) leans Dallas. Sukru's personal interests, Marina, and Maja all lean Philly.

**The tension:** Dallas has the better match and more kid attractions (theme parks, soccer exhibit), but it's hotter, harder to navigate, and a bigger logistical lift. Philly is the easier, more rewarding city to explore as an adult, more comfortable for the family, and a better fit for Maja -- but with a less exciting game and no theme parks.

**One way to resolve it:** Go to **both**. Dallas is June 17, Philly is June 27 -- ten days apart. Or if picking one:

- **Match is the priority** -> Dallas
- **Family trip is the priority** -> Philadelphia
- **Your personal enjoyment of the city** -> Philadelphia
