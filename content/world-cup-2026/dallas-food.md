Anchored to the **Residence Inn Arlington** (1050 Brookhollow Plaza Dr, Arlington, TX 76006), in the Arlington Entertainment District near AT&T Stadium, Globe Life Field, and Six Flags. The scene runs the full Texas gamut -- world-class BBQ, frozen-margarita-birthplace Tex-Mex, chicken-fried-steak feasts, and a serious Dallas/Fort Worth fine-dining bench -- but the genuinely great stuff is mostly a drive, since the immediate hotel area is chains and stroad strip centers. Prices span $ (Whataburger, frozen custard) to $$$$ (Michelin-starred omakase, tower-view steakhouses), with the everyday family sweet spot at $$ ($12-30/pp).

**Two honest caveats up front:** (1) Driving + walking distances below are **MapKit-measured from the hotel** (a few multi-location chains kept estimates and are flagged "verify branch") -- still **double-check day-of**, and pad heavily for World Cup match-day traffic and road closures around the I-30/stadium corridor (matches June 14-July 14). (2) BBQ joints sell out before posted close; Texas Live! flips 21+ after 9pm -- dine early with kids.

## Destination / worth the trip

Iconic Texas spots and the metro's serious fine-dining bench -- all a 15-40 min drive into Fort Worth or Dallas (Fort Worth is meaningfully closer). The Arlington Entertainment District has no nationally notable fine dining within walking distance; **every pick here is a drive.**

### Iconic Texas (Fort Worth / Dallas / Frisco)

⭐ **Joe T. Garcia's** -- Tex-Mex (iconic) | $$ (~$18-28/pp) | ~20-22 mi / 25-30 min drive (Fort Worth) | Kid-friendly: very good -- sprawling garden patios with fountains and a pool; simple menu suits kids
- Signature: the legendary family-style dinner -- cheese enchiladas and beef fajitas with rice, beans, handmade tortillas; some of Texas's most famous margaritas
- Hours: Mon-Thu 11am-2:30pm & 5-10pm, Fri-Sat 11am-11pm, Sun 11am-10pm. **CASH ONLY (on-site ATMs).** No reservations -- lines form but the big space moves fast
- Why: a 1935 Fort Worth institution and bucket-list Tex-Mex patio -- pair with a Stockyards visit. *Confirm you have cash*
- https://www.joetgarcias.com/

⭐ **Goldee's Barbecue** -- Texas BBQ | $$ (~$20-35/pp, by weight) | ~15-17 mi / 22-25 min drive (SE Fort Worth) | Kid-friendly: moderate -- food is very kid-edible but the weekend-only line tests young kids' patience

- Why: widely called the best brisket in Texas -- the serious pilgrimage stop if you can do a weekend morning and tolerate a line
- https://guide.michelin.com/us/en/texas/fort-worth_2954653/restaurant/goldee-s

⭐ **Heim Barbecue (River District)** -- Texas BBQ | $$ (~$18-30/pp) | ~22-24 mi / 28-32 min drive (Fort Worth) | Kid-friendly: very good -- full table service and a patio (not line-only), longer dinner hours
