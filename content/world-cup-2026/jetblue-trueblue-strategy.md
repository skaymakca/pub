## What is cpp (cents per point)?

**cpp** measures how much cash value you get per point when redeeming. It's calculated as:

> **cpp = (cash fare − points cash copay) ÷ points used**

For example, an $1,156 roundtrip that costs 82,700 pts + $569.56 copay: ($1,156 − $569.56) / 82,700 = **0.71 cpp**.

Higher cpp = better value. JetBlue TrueBlue points are commonly valued at ~1.3-1.5 cpp based on **search page estimates**, but actual checkout copay is much higher than the search-page "$5.60 per segment" display suggests. Real-world cpp depends on the specific booking.

**Important:** JetBlue search results show points prices as "X pts + $5.60" -- but $5.60 is only the displayed government security fee. At actual checkout, the cash copay includes taxes, facility charges, and carrier fees that are substantially higher. Always verify at checkout before committing.
