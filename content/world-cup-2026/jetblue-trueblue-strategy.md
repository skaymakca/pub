## What is cpp (cents per point)?

**cpp** measures how much cash value you get per point when redeeming. It's calculated as:

> **cpp = cash price ÷ points price**

For example, a $135 flight costing 9,300 points: $135 / 9,300 = **1.45 cpp** — each point is worth 1.45 cents.

Higher cpp = better value for your points. JetBlue TrueBlue points are generally worth ~1.3–1.5 cpp. Anything above 1.4 cpp is a good redemption. Below 1.3 cpp, you're better off paying cash and saving points for a higher-value use.

The $5.60 fee per segment is charged regardless of redemption method and does not factor into the cpp calculation.
