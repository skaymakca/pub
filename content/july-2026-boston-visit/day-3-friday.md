### What's on Georges Island

**Fort Warren** (1833-1860) is a National Historic Landmark, a Union training camp, and a Confederate prison -- it held the Confederate Vice President. A real granite fort you walk into and around, not a reconstruction behind glass.

- ⭐ **The Dark Tunnel** -- an actual unlit passage visitors walk through. **Bring a flashlight.** This is the thing they'll still be talking about in October.
- ⭐ **Junior Ranger program** -- free booklet from the visitor center, complete the activities, earn an official badge. Both kids are in range, and it gives them a reason to read the signage instead of ignoring it.
- **The Lady in Black** -- the fort's resident ghost legend. Ask a ranger; they tell it properly.
- Parade ground, bakery ruins, playground, visitor center, restrooms, drinking water.

[!] **No swimming on Georges.** The fort has steep drop-offs and narrow unlit staircases -- fine at 10 and 12, but not a place to let kids roam entirely unsupervised.

### Ferry facts (verified July 2026)

**Operator:** Boston Harbor City Cruises. Tickets 617-227-4321; refunds (866) 307-2469.

**Season:** summer schedule runs **June 15 - September 7, seven days a week**.

| | Departs Boston | Returns from island | Crossing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Georges Island** | 10:00, 12:00, 2:00 | 11:45, 1:45, 3:45, 5:45 | ~45 min |
| **Spectacle Island** | 10:30, 12:30, 2:30 | 12:00, 2:00, 4:00, 6:00 | ~30 min |

**Tickets are round-trip and you don't select a return time**, so they can come back on whichever boat suits how the day is going. That flexibility is worth a lot with kids.

**Food is handled** -- ZaZ Restaurant is the 2026 concessioner on both Georges and Spectacle (Caribbean fusion). Packing lunch is optional; picnic tables are everywhere.

**Pricing:** the park site still displays **2025** fares -- Adult $24.95, Child $17.95, Senior $22.95. Budget roughly **$85 for the four of them**, expect a modest bump.

[!] The site shows a garbled *"Child Under 35 Ride Pass: Free"* -- almost certainly "under 3" plus a separate 5-ride pass mangled by a layout bug. Needs a phone call, and it decides whether the 10-year-old pays.

[!] **No inter-island shuttle appears on the 2026 schedule.** There was historically a Georges ↔ Spectacle hop; plan on **one island per day** unless confirmed otherwise.

### The other islands, for reference

**Spectacle Island** -- the beach-and-views option: designated swimming beach (**unguarded**), 1.5-mile ADA perimeter loop, North Drumlin overlook, summer jazz. Very little shade. Better *hot day* island, decisively weaker *history* island.

**Peddocks Island** -- skip. Ferry access is from **Hingham**, not Boston, and there's no food service.

## Plan B -- Museum of Science + Harvard Square

The rain-proof version. Green Line from Copley to Science Park (stay on the same line, ride through downtown). Museum of Science in the morning, 3-4 hours. Then Red Line to Harvard for the Square and the ⭐ **Glass Flowers**.

Use this if Friday is wet -- Fort Warren in the rain is a miserable proposition and the ferry is exposed.

## Plan C -- Charlestown, self-guided

If they'd rather have Sunday relaxed, they can take the Freedom Trail's far end themselves: **Bunker Hill Monument** (294 steps) and **USS Constitution**. Green Line to Government Center, then walk or take the ferry across.

[!] If they do this, **photo ID is required for 18+** at the Navy Yard checkpoint, and it takes Charlestown off Sunday -- which frees Sunday for a slower morning before the procession.
