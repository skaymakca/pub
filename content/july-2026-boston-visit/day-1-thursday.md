## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Geography** | Boston Common → downtown → North End. All on foot, roughly 1.5 miles end to end. |
| **Who** | All of us |
| **Transit** | Walking. Green Line D in, no car. |
| **Fixed point** | Feast opens **7:00pm**, children's procession **7:30pm**, Hanover Street |
| **Shape** | Entirely dependent on when they land |

The day does two jobs: give the kids their first hit of Boston history on foot, and land everyone on Hanover Street as the feast opens. The Freedom Trail's downtown half is the spine, and it conveniently dead-ends in the North End, so the walk delivers you exactly where the evening is.

**Pick the plan that matches their arrival time.** Everything below A is a compression of A, not a different day.

---

## ⭐ Plan A -- lands before ~1pm

The full downtown half of the Freedom Trail, unhurried, with time to actually stop.

**Boston Common** -- start at the visitor center and pick up the trail. Oldest public park in the country (1634), which is the sort of fact a 12-year-old will fact-check you on.

**Granary Burying Ground** -- Paul Revere, John Hancock, Sam Adams, and the victims of the Boston Massacre, all in one small yard. The colonial gravestones are carved with winged skulls and hourglasses -- genuinely strange, genuinely morbid, and reliably the stop that converts a kid who thought this trip was going to be dull. Budget more time here than seems reasonable.

**Old State House** -- the oldest surviving public building in Boston, and the Boston Massacre happened on the traffic island directly outside it. Take them out to the marker and stand on it. The physical spot does more work than the exhibit inside does.

**Faneuil Hall / Quincy Market** -- the colonnade food hall is open and running roughly 35 stalls. Treat it as a snack and a bathroom stop, not a meal; dinner is in the North End.

**Cross the Greenway into the North End** and you're there.

---

## Plan B -- lands mid-afternoon

Drop the Common and the Granary. Start at the **Old State House**, walk to **Faneuil Hall**, and be in the North End by 5.

You lose nothing permanently -- Sunday brings you back downtown, and the Granary can be picked up then.

---

## Plan C -- lands after 6pm

Go straight to Hanover Street. Eat at the feast itself, catch the children's procession, and call it a night. Jet-lagged kids plus a street festival is a fine first evening and requires no plan at all.

---

## The Evening

**7:00pm** -- opening ceremony. **7:30pm** -- the children's procession. A 10- and 12-year-old are precisely the target audience, and it's the single most "you are actually in this neighborhood, not visiting it" thing available all week.

### ⭐ The cannoli bit

Buy one cannoli from **Mike's Pastry** (300 Hanover) and one from **Modern Pastry** (257 Hanover), then run a blind taste test and let the kids render the verdict.

The Mike's-versus-Modern rivalry is a real and unresolved local schism -- people have positions on this. Handing children the authority to settle it is considerably more fun than either pastry, and it gives them something to be smug about for the rest of the trip. Both are walk-in and cash-friendly.

---

## Eat

Eat early and casually -- you want to be on Hanover Street by 7, not waiting on a check.

**Fun -- ⭐ Regina Pizzeria (the original)**
- 📍 11½ Thacher St, North End · walk-in · $ · cash-friendly
- The original only. The mall and airport locations are a completely different experience and do the place a disservice. Brick oven, tiny room, no reservations, fast enough to make a 7pm procession.

**Good -- Carmelina's**
- 📍 Hanover St, North End · limited reservations / walk-in · $$$
- Michelin-recommended in 2025. The North End pick that's genuinely about the food rather than the atmosphere, and it's steps from the feast.

**Backup -- Bova's Bakery**
- 📍 134 Salem St · open 24 hours, since 1926 · $
- For the inevitable second dessert, or if the day falls apart and you need food at an odd hour.

---

## If it rains

The feast runs rain or shine and the procession still goes. Compress to **Plan C**, skip the walking, and treat it as a food-and-street-festival evening. The Freedom Trail's downtown half can move to Sunday morning, which is the one other day that puts you downtown.
