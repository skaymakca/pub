## Two Categories, Deliberately Separate

**"Good"** means go for the cooking. The room might be plain, the location inconvenient, the wait annoying -- you're there because the food is excellent.

**"Fun" / worth-seeing** means go for the room, the history, the institution-ness, the line out the door. Food quality is secondary and sometimes frankly beside the point. Union Oyster House is not the best seafood in Boston. It is 200 years old, and that's the reason.

The two lists barely overlap, which is why they're kept apart here rather than blended into one ranked list. Picks are spread deliberately across neighborhoods -- North End, Cambridge, Somerville, South End, Southie, Dorchester, Chinatown, East Boston -- rather than clustered downtown.

Each day page carries its own local picks. This is the full menu to draw from.

## Fun / Worth-Seeing

**⭐ Union Oyster House** -- 📍 41 Union St, downtown · $$$ · OpenTable
**Turned 200 in 2026.** Oldest continuously operating restaurant in America. Sit at the ground-floor semicircular raw bar where Daniel Webster drank; the bar is the historic artifact, not the upstairs dining rooms.

**⭐ Regina Pizzeria (original)** -- 📍 11½ Thacher St, North End · $ · walk-in, cash-friendly
The original only -- the mall and airport branches are a different business wearing the same name. Brick oven, small room, fast.

**⭐ Sullivan's Castle Island** -- 📍 2080 Day Blvd, South Boston · $ · walk-up window
**75th season, and it just won a James Beard America's Classics award.** Hot dogs and fried clams at a window on the water. Pair it with a walk around Castle Island and Fort Independence -- it's a genuinely great couple of hours and almost entirely free.

**Mike's Pastry vs Modern Pastry** -- 📍 300 and 257 Hanover St · $ · walk-in, cash
Buy one cannoli from each and let the kids blind-taste them. The rivalry is a real local schism; the verdict is the entertainment.

**Mr. Bartley's Burger Cottage** -- 📍 1246 Mass Ave, Harvard Sq · $ · **walk-in only, historically cash-only**
Burgers named after politicians, walls of accumulated clutter, frappes and onion rings.

**Barking Crab** -- 📍 88 Sleeper St, Seaport · $$ · walk-in
Picnic tables, paper bibs, harbor views. Loud, and good with kids.

**Hei La Moon** -- 📍 83 Essex St, Chinatown · $$ · walk-in
**Cart** dim sum -- the carts are the entire point for kids, who get to choose by pointing. Go before noon.

**Santarpio's** -- 📍 111 Chelsea St, East Boston · $ · walk-in
No frills, no salad. Pizza and barbecue skewers in an unchanged room.

**China Pearl** -- 📍 Chinatown · $$ · walk-in
Reopened after roughly five years of renovation. [!] The dim sum floor is **stairs-only**.

**Bova's Bakery** -- 📍 134 Salem St, North End · $ · **open 24 hours**, since 1926
The odd-hours fallback and the second-dessert solution.

**Toscanini's** (899 Main / 159 First) and **Christina's** (1255 Cambridge St, Inman) -- $ · Cambridge
The two best ice cream shops in the area. Christina's has the wilder flavor list.

## Context: Boston Got a Michelin Guide

**Michelin published its first Boston guide in November 2025.** If the parents last visited before that, the city's dining conversation has moved underneath them and it's worth mentioning.

The only **starred** restaurant is **311 Omakase** in the South End -- roughly $350-400 per person, its own booking system releasing 75 days out, and emphatically not a kids' night. Noted for completeness rather than as a recommendation for this trip.

Also new since a pre-2025 visit:

- **Dalia** -- 429 W Broadway, Southie. Wood-fired Spanish, opened April 2026. Hard to book.
- **Kaia** -- 380 Harrison Ave, South End. Modern Aegean, **walk-in only**, Condé Nast Hot List.
- **La Padrona** -- Raffles Hotel, Back Bay. Regional Italian under Jody Adams. **2026 James Beard semifinalist.** Formal setting, but the pasta menu works for kids.
- **Lê Madeline** -- North Quincy. Modern Vietnamese, tableside wagyu pho. Globe four stars, April 2026. Outside the city but genuinely kid-friendly.
