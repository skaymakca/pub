The busiest day of the trip, and the one with moving parts: a tour, a checkout, a drive, and a change of address. It works because of one piece of luck -- **the Duck Tour departs from the Prudential Center, which is a five-minute walk from their hotel** (they're linked by skybridge).

So: ride the ducks, walk back, check out, load the car, drive to Lexington. No wasted movement.

---

## ⭐ Plan A -- ducks in the morning, the Revolution in the afternoon

### Boston Duck Tours -- Prudential Center

📍 **53 Huntington Ave** -- five minutes on foot from the Westin, or dry via the skybridge.

Amphibious WWII-style landing craft that tour the city on wheels and then drive **straight into the Charles River**. Unashamedly touristy; kids love them without reservation, and **our kids specifically asked for this one**, which makes it the right call regardless of how it scores on a purist's itinerary.

Roughly 80 minutes. Aim for a **9:00 or 9:30 departure** so there's room before checkout.

[!] **Book ahead**, and confirm 2026 departure times and pricing -- I verified the three departure locations (Prudential Center, Museum of Science, New England Aquarium) but the site doesn't publish times or fares on its public pages.

### Checkout and transfer

Back at the hotel by ~11:00. Standard checkout is noon; bags into the car, and everyone's luggage rides to Newton with us rather than being schlepped around Concord.

**Lexington is about 30 minutes from Copley.** Rolling by 12:15 puts you on the Battle Green around 12:45.

### ⭐ Lexington and Concord

The afternoon follows April 19, 1775 **in sequence**, which is what makes it work for kids -- it's a story with a beginning and an end, not a series of unrelated plaques.

**Lexington Battle Green** -- where it started. Eight colonists killed in the first exchange. The Green is small and unimpressive-looking, which is precisely the point worth making to a 12-year-old: this is what the start of a revolution actually looks like. **Buckman Tavern** faces it, where the militia waited through the night.

⭐ **Minute Man NHP -- Hartwell Tavern.** The target of the day. When staffed, interpreters in period dress fire muskets and talk through the events. **This is the difference between reading about the Revolution and hearing it** -- a musket is startlingly loud, and that lands in a way no exhibit does.

**Battle Road Trail** -- walk a stretch of the actual road the British retreated along under fire for sixteen miles. A mile out and back near Hartwell is plenty; it's wooded and shaded, which matters in August.

**The North Bridge, Concord** -- "the shot heard round the world," and a short, genuinely pretty walk down to the bridge and the Minute Man statue.

⭐ **Walden Pond** -- end here and swim. Early-August heat makes this the right closer regardless, and it's Thoreau, so it counts as literary pilgrimage while being, to a 10-year-old, simply a very good pond. The replica cabin and the cairn are a ten-minute stop; the swimming is the reason to come.

Then home to Newton.

[!] **Walden's parking lot fills early on hot summer days and they turn cars away.** Check the reservation system -- this is the item most likely to quietly ruin the end of the day.

### What gets cut

The morning tour compresses the afternoon, so **assume Orchard House comes off**. It was already conditional -- worth it only if the 12-year-old has read *Little Women* or is close. It's a talky house tour with timed small-group tickets, and it will die on a kid with no investment in it.

If they *have* read it and want it, the trade is Battle Road: do Lexington, Hartwell, North Bridge, Orchard House, and skip the trail walk.

## Plan B -- move the Duck Tour to Sunday

If the morning feels too tight, the tour also departs from the **New England Aquarium (Central Wharf)**, and a Sunday-morning ride ends within a 12-minute walk of the North End -- so they could ride at 9:00 and be on Hanover Street for the 12:30 procession.

**The cost:** it takes Charlestown off Sunday, which means dropping **USS Constitution and Bunker Hill**. That's a real loss and it's why this is Plan B rather than Plan A -- the Duck Tour is replaceable on a future visit; boarding the oldest commissioned warship afloat is less so.

Take this if Saturday morning turns out to be a scramble.

---

## Plan C -- skip Lexington, do Concord only

If the Duck Tour runs long or checkout drags. Drive straight to the **North Bridge** and **Walden**, skipping Lexington Green and Battle Road. Loses the sequence but keeps the two best physical places, and buys back an hour and a half.

---

## If it rains

**This is the one day that genuinely needs weather.** Battle Road in rain is a wet walk in the woods and Walden is pointless.

Best swap: trade Saturday and Friday -- give them the Museum of Science on Friday and take Concord on a dry day. Failing that, the Duck Tour still runs in rain (the boats have roofs and it's funnier), and the **Museum of Fine Arts** or the ⭐ **Isabella Stewart Gardner** make a good indoor afternoon. The Gardner is the better story for kids: a Venetian palace built inside Boston, site of the largest unsolved art heist in history, and **the empty frames still hang on the walls** where the stolen paintings were.
