## Why this page exists

The itinerary was originally built history-first, on the strength of "historic sites and local culture." **We don't actually know how much history these guests want**, and a 10- and 12-year-old who aren't into it will make that obvious by hour two of a walking tour.

So: this page is the other track. Everything here is a real alternative to a history day, and each day page now points at it. **They do like sports**, which turns out to be awkward timing -- but not a dead end.

**One thing stays fixed regardless:** we're all doing **USS Constitution** together on Sunday. Boarding the oldest commissioned warship afloat is worth it even for someone indifferent to history, and it's the one historic thing on the list that isn't a walking tour.

## The Sports Museum at TD Garden

📍 TD Garden, North Station · **open daily 10:30am - 5:00pm**, tours depart **11:00am - 4:30pm**

This is what "a tour of the Garden" means in practice. Celtics and Bruins history, championship banners, memorabilia. Standard tours, private VIP tours, and field-trip formats are offered.

[!] **Admission prices are not published** on the site -- call ahead. Tour duration also unstated.

**Where it fits:** North Station sits between Charlestown and the North End, so it *could* slot into Sunday between USS Constitution and the 12:30 procession -- but that morning is tight, and Sunday now forks between the ship and the Duck Tour, so there isn't really room. Treat the Sports Museum as a standalone or rainy-day option rather than a scheduled stop.

## ⚠️ Live games -- worth checking, I could not verify

**These are the highest-value unknowns on the whole trip.** Both `milb.com` and `woosox.com` block automated access, so I could not confirm any of the following. **Please check these directly** -- a live game would be a genuinely great evening and beats every museum on this page.

### ⭐ Worcester Red Sox (WooSox) -- the best bet

Triple-A affiliate of the Red Sox at **Polar Park, Worcester**. Roughly **40-45 minutes west of Newton on the Pike** -- and critically, *west*, which is the same direction as nothing else on the itinerary, so it's a clean standalone evening.

Minor league baseball is arguably **better than the majors for kids this age**: cheap tickets, close to the field, constant between-innings silliness, frequent fireworks nights, and actual Red Sox prospects. It's a real "local Sox game" in every sense that matters to a 10-year-old.

**Check:** home games at Polar Park Jul 29 - Aug 2, and whether any is a fireworks night.

### New England Revolution -- MLS

Gillette Stadium, Foxborough, ~40 min south. The MLS season is active in early August. **Check for a home match that week.**

### Boston Legacy FC -- NWSL

Boston's new NWSL club, inaugural season 2026, playing at White Stadium in Franklin Park. Much closer than Gillette and a genuinely novel thing to have seen. **Check the schedule** -- I could not confirm dates or whether the venue is fully open.

### Cape Cod Baseball League

Free admission, elite college summer ball, playoffs in early August. Charming and completely unpretentious -- but the Cape is 1.5-2 hours each way, which makes it a whole day rather than an evening.

## Non-history days -- the full menu

Ranked roughly by how well they'd land with a 10- and 12-year-old.

### ⭐ Whale watch

The single best non-history family activity in Boston, and it departs from **Long Wharf / Central Wharf** -- the same dock as the Georges Island ferry and next to the Aquarium.

Three to four hours out to Stellwagen Bank. Humpbacks, finbacks, minkes. Operated in partnership between the New England Aquarium and Boston Harbor City Cruises.

[!] 2026 times and prices **not verified** -- the operator's page is a template with placeholders. Call 888-534-3516. Also worth knowing: it's a long trip on open water, so it's a bad idea for anyone prone to seasickness.

**Where it fits:** straight swap for the island on **Friday**, keeping the same geography and the Aquarium afternoon.

### ⭐ New England Aquarium

📍 Central Wharf · **open daily 9:00am - 6:00pm** (verified)

The giant ocean tank spirals four stories through the middle of the building. Penguins, rays, seals. Reliable, indoor, and four minutes from the ferry dock -- it's already the afternoon half of Friday's plan.

### ⭐ Museum of Science

Best straight-up kid museum in the city, and 10 and 12 is a good age spread for it. Lightning show, Omni theater. Green Line straight to Science Park. Absorbs a full rainy day.

### Boston Duck Tours

Already on the schedule for Saturday morning. Amphibious, splashes into the Charles, and our kids asked for it. Departs Prudential Center (five minutes from their hotel), Museum of Science, or the Aquarium.

### Castle Island + Sullivan's

South Boston. A walkable fort on the water, a beach, planes landing overhead, and ⭐ **Sullivan's** -- 75th season, walk-up window, **just won a James Beard America's Classics award**. Hot dogs and fried clams. Almost entirely free and genuinely lovely on a hot afternoon.

### Charles River kayaking

Paddle Boston rents from several points including a **Newton location on the Charles**. Low-commitment, close to home, good for a spare afternoon.

### Franklin Park Zoo

Solid, not spectacular. Better for younger kids than these two, but a fine rainy-ish fallback.

### Revere Beach

Blue Line straight there. The first public beach in America, which is the only history involved. Good on a brutal day.

## Where these fit against the day plans

Most of these are now folded into the day pages. In short:

| Day | Plan | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| **Wed** | Back Bay walk | *locked* |
| **Thu** | Freedom Trail + feast | *locked* |
| **Fri** | Museum of Science + Harvard | whale watch or Georges Island; **SPIN + Seaport** evening |
| **Sat** | Lexington & Concord | ⭐ **WooSox game**, Castle Island, or a park day |
| **Sun** | ⭐ **USS Constitution** | **Duck Tour** (kids' pick) -- pick one |

The **Fenway tour** and **Sports Museum** aren't slotted into a specific day -- drop either into any open morning. A **WooSox / Revolution / Legacy FC** game is the Saturday fork if the schedule cooperates.

**The feast needs no interest in history.** It's a street festival with food, bands, and a procession -- nobody experiences it as a lesson.

**Georges Island sits between the tracks too.** It reads as a boat ride, an island, and an unlit tunnel you walk through with a flashlight. The Civil War fort is the setting, not the homework.
