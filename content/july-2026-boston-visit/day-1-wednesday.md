They land mid-afternoon, check in, and have an evening. **This day is deliberately unambitious** -- they've flown, the kids will be fried, and the entire point is to get outside, stay awake until a reasonable hour, and start on Boston time.

Everything here is walkable from the hotel, needs no tickets, and can be abandoned at any point without wasting anything.

**Getting from Logan:** with luggage and two kids, a taxi or rideshare is worth it -- roughly 20-30 minutes to Copley depending on the tunnel. The transit alternative is the Silver Line (free from the airport) to South Station, then the Red Line to Park and the Green Line out to Copley -- cheap, but two transfers with bags on arrival day is a bad trade.

## ⭐ Plan A -- the Back Bay walk

### Boston Public Library, McKim Building

Directly across Copley Square from the hotel, and **free**.

This is one of the great interiors in America and almost nobody tells visitors to go. **Bates Hall** is a 218-foot barrel-vaulted reading room; the **courtyard** is an Italianate cloister with a fountain in the middle of the city; the Sargent murals are upstairs. It's cool, quiet, and about four minutes from where they dropped their bags -- close to ideal for a jet-lagged first stop.

[!] Confirm evening hours before counting on it -- summer hours vary and it closes earlier than you'd expect on some days.

### Copley Square and Trinity Church

The square itself, with **Trinity Church** on the east side -- Richardson's Romanesque masterpiece, and the building the library was designed to answer across the open space. Worth five minutes of looking even without going in.

### Newbury Street

Eight blocks of brownstone retail running from the Public Garden to Mass Ave. It's the standard Back Bay stroll and it's genuinely pleasant on a summer evening.

### ⭐ The Public Garden

About 15 minutes on foot, and the right place to end up.

The **swan boats** close in the late afternoon so they'll likely miss riding them -- but the real draw for kids this age is the **Make Way for Ducklings** bronzes near the Charles Street corner: Mrs. Mallard and eight ducklings in a row, usually dressed in whatever the city has put on them that week. Both kids are probably a touch old for the book and will enjoy them anyway.

Nice bit of foreshadowing, too -- they'll be on an actual duck boat Saturday morning.

## Plan B -- if they're wrecked

Skybridges connect the Westin to **Copley Place** and the **Prudential Center**, so there's a whole indoor world attached to the hotel with food, without going outside at all. Entirely reasonable on a travel day.

The **Prudential Skywalk Observatory** is in the same complex if they want a view without a walk. (Confirm current hours and whether it's ticketed.)

## Eat -- Back Bay, walkable

They're on their own tonight, so these are picks that survive a jet-lagged table.

**Good -- Krasi**
- 📍 Back Bay · OpenTable · $$$
- Greek small plates and the largest Greek wine list in the country. Shared plates are forgiving when nobody can agree on what they want.

**Good -- Select Oyster Bar**
- 📍 Back Bay · OpenTable · $$$
- Small, seafood-focused, very good. Borderline for kids on a busy night -- it's a tight room.

**Zero-effort fallback** -- the food options inside **Copley Place** and the **Prudential Center** are reachable without stepping outdoors via the skybridges. Not a destination meal, but on an arrival night with a 10-year-old fading fast, that's the correct call and nobody should feel bad about it.
