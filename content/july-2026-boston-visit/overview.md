## The Situation

Friends are visiting **Thursday July 30 through Sunday August 2, 2026**, flying out Monday the 3rd. Four usable days. They're staying with us in **Newton Centre**, so lodging isn't part of the plan -- this is purely activities, food, and daily routing.

**The party:** two parents plus kids aged **10 and 12**. The parents have been to Boston before; the kids haven't. That combination sets the whole approach: hit the classics, but pick the *versions* of them that play for a 10- and 12-year-old rather than the standard adult tourist rendering.

**What they asked for:** historic sites and local culture. Restaurants matter too, in two deliberately separate categories -- **"good"** (go for the cooking) and **"fun"** (go for the room, the history, the line out the door), spread across the city and Cambridge rather than clustered downtown.

**Our involvement:** with them most days, but Friday is a workday, so that one is built to be self-guided.

## The Two Fixed Points

Everything else bends around these.

### ⭐ The Feast of St. Agrippina di Mineo -- Thu Jul 30 to Sun Aug 2

A 100+ year old Sicilian-American street feast on **Hanover Street in the North End**. Free. It opens the night they land and peaks on their last full day, which is an absurd piece of luck -- it brackets the visit with no scheduling effort at all. This is not a tourist product; it's the neighborhood's own thing.

Two moments matter:

| When | What |
|------|------|
| **Thu 7/30, 7:00pm** | Opening ceremony, followed by the **7:30pm children's procession** |
| **Sun 8/2, 12:30pm** | **Grand Procession** -- the saint carried through the streets, money pinned to the ribbons, brass bands. Closes with Mass at St. Leonard's and fireworks. |

Friday and Saturday the feast opens around noon with DJs and live bands -- worth a drop-in but not worth building a day around.

[!] **Confirm the dates by phone before committing Thursday night.** The official site's header reads "July 30 - Aug 2, 2026" but its day-by-day schedule block still carries 2025's date numbers. The day-of-week pattern is internally consistent and that's what's mapped here, but the site is visibly half-updated.

### The Red Sox are away all four days

## How the days swap

Days are geographically self-contained, so most of them can trade places without breaking anything. **One exception, and it's worth understanding before anyone gets clever with the calendar:**

Saturday is Lexington & Concord *on purpose*. Hartwell Tavern's living-history programming is weekend-weighted -- Saturday is when the costumed interpreters are out and the muskets actually fire. Moving Concord off Saturday means either taking a weekday and losing the demonstrations, or reaching it by commuter rail, which gets you Orchard House and Walden but **strands Minute Man National Historical Park entirely**. That park was the point.

So: swap Friday and Saturday freely if the weather demands it. Just don't quietly relocate Concord to a weekday.

## Practical

**MBTA fares.** Kids **11 and under ride free** across bus, subway, Commuter Rail and ferry -- so the **10-year-old rides free and the 12-year-old pays full fare**. The Youth Pass is a registered-resident program and almost certainly not obtainable by a visiting kid, so budget adult fare for the 12-year-old.

**Parking.** Don't drive downtown any day. The car comes out Saturday only.

**Packing, by day.** Flashlight for the Dark Tunnel at Fort Warren (Friday), sunscreen and water for the island, **photo ID for the adults on Sunday** -- USS Constitution runs a Navy Yard security checkpoint and requires it for anyone 18+. That one catches people out.

**Weather.** Early August in Boston is hot and humid. Two days end near water on purpose (Walden on Saturday, the harbor on Friday). The Bunker Hill climb closes in extreme heat, which is why Sunday starts early.
