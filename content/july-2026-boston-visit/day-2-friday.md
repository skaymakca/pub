## ⭐ Plan A -- Georges Island + the Aquarium

This works because of one piece of geography: **the ferry dock and the New England Aquarium are the same place.** Long Wharf North sits directly beside the Aquarium, so a full day happens with almost no transit inside it.

### The shape of it

| Time | |
|------|---|
| **8:45am** | Leave Newton Centre. Green Line D → Government Center, Blue Line one stop → Aquarium. About 45-50 minutes, one transfer. |
| **10:00am** | Ferry departs **Long Wharf North, 66 Long Wharf** |
| **10:45am** | Land on Georges Island |
| **10:45 - 1:45** | Fort Warren, the Dark Tunnel, Junior Ranger badge, lunch on-island |
| **1:45pm** | Return ferry, docks around 2:30 |
| **Afternoon** | **New England Aquarium** -- a four-minute walk from where they step off the boat |
| **Evening** | The North End is a 12-minute walk up the Greenway if they want dinner in town |

### What's actually on Georges Island

**Fort Warren** (built 1833-1860) is a National Historic Landmark, a Union training camp, and a Confederate prison -- it held the Confederate Vice President. It's a real granite fort you walk into and around, not a reconstruction behind glass.

- ⭐ **The Dark Tunnel** -- an actual unlit passage that visitors walk through. **Bring a flashlight.** This is the thing a 10- and 12-year-old will still be talking about in October.
- ⭐ **Junior Ranger program** -- free booklet from the visitor center, complete the activities, earn an official badge. Both kids are comfortably in range, and it gives them a reason to read the interpretive signage instead of ignoring it.
- **The Lady in Black** -- the fort's resident ghost legend. Ask a ranger; they tell it properly.
- Parade ground, bakery ruins, a playground, visitor center, restrooms, drinking water.

[!] **No swimming on Georges.** The fort also has steep drop-offs and narrow unlit staircases -- fine at 10 and 12, but not a place to let kids roam entirely unsupervised.

### Ferry facts (verified July 2026)

**Operator:** Boston Harbor City Cruises. Tickets 617-227-4321; refunds (866) 307-2469.

**Season:** the summer schedule runs **June 15 - September 7, seven days a week**, so the whole visit sits comfortably inside it.

| | Departs Boston | Returns from island | Crossing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Georges Island** | 10:00, 12:00, 2:00 | 11:45, 1:45, 3:45, 5:45 | ~45 min |
| **Spectacle Island** | 10:30, 12:30, 2:30 | 12:00, 2:00, 4:00, 6:00 | ~30 min |

**All tickets are round-trip and you don't select a return time** -- so they can come back on whichever boat suits how the day is going. That flexibility is worth a lot with kids.

**Food is handled.** ZaZ Restaurant is the 2026 concessioner on both Georges and Spectacle (Caribbean fusion), so packing lunch is optional. Picnic tables are everywhere if they'd rather.

**Pricing:** the park site still displays **2025** fares -- Adult $24.95, Child $17.95, Senior $22.95. Budget roughly **$85 for the four of them** and expect a modest 2026 bump.

[!] The site lists a garbled *"Child Under 35 Ride Pass: Free"*, which is almost certainly "under 3 rides free" and a separate 5-ride pass mashed together by a layout bug. It needs a human on the phone, and it determines whether the 10-year-old pays.

[!] **No inter-island shuttle appears on the 2026 schedule.** There was historically a Georges ↔ Spectacle hop. Its absence means plan on **one island per day** unless confirmed otherwise.

### Why Friday beats Saturday for the island

Weekday ferries are quieter and far less likely to sell out, and Fort Warren is a much better experience when you aren't queueing to get through the tunnel. Early-August Saturday boats do sell out.

### The other island, for reference

**Spectacle Island** is the beach-and-views option: a designated swimming beach (**unguarded**), a 1.5-mile ADA-accessible perimeter loop, the North Drumlin overlook, and summer jazz. Very little shade. It's the better *hot day* island but decisively the weaker *history* island -- which is why Georges wins for these guests.

**Peddocks Island** (Fort Andrews) -- skip. Ferry access is from **Hingham**, not Boston, and there's no food service at all.

## Plan B -- Museum of Science + Harvard Square

The rain-proof version, and the single easiest day to navigate: **the Green Line D runs Newton Centre straight through to Science Park with no transfer at all.** The museum is at the stop.

**Morning: Museum of Science.** The best straight-up kid museum in the city, and 10 and 12 is a good age spread for it -- enough range that neither kid is bored. Budget 3-4 hours.

**Afternoon: Cambridge.** Green Line back to Park, Red Line to Harvard.

- **Harvard Square** -- wander it, bookshops and street performers
- ⭐ **Harvard Museum of Natural History** -- the **Glass Flowers**. Hand-blown glass botanical models so exact that visitors routinely refuse to believe they aren't real plants. It's a strange, quiet, genuinely astonishing collection and it lands with this age group far more often than it has any right to.

## Plan C -- Duck Tour + Back Bay

If Thursday collapsed into its Plan C and they never got an orientation pass at the city. The Duck Tours are amphibious WWII landing craft that drive the city and then splash into the Charles. Unashamedly touristy; kids love them anyway.

Pair with the Public Garden swan boats and a walk up Newbury Street.

---

## If it rains

**Plan B, unchanged** -- it's built for this. Museum of Science and the Harvard museums absorb a wet day completely.

If it rains Friday *and* Saturday looks clear, consider swapping the two days entirely: Concord is the one day that genuinely needs weather.

## Eat

They're on their own, so these are picks they can execute without a reservation or a plan.

**Fun -- ⭐ Mr. Bartley's Burger Cottage**
- 📍 1246 Mass Ave, Harvard Square · walk-in only · $
- Burgers named after politicians, walls covered in decades of accumulated clutter. **Historically cash-only.** Get a frappe and onion rings. Perfect if they end up on Plan B.

**Fun -- Barking Crab**
- 📍 88 Sleeper St, Seaport · walk-in · $$
- Picnic tables, paper bibs, harbor views. A short walk from Long Wharf, so it's the natural Plan A dinner if they stay downtown.

**Good -- ⭐ Giulia**
- 📍 Porter Square, Cambridge · OpenTable · $$$
- Hand-rolled pasta, and they roll it on a big table in the middle of the dining room. The best of the "good" list for kids, because there's something to watch.

**Ice cream -- Toscanini's** (899 Main / 159 First) or **Christina's** (1255 Cambridge St, Inman Square). Christina's has the wilder flavor list; both are worth the detour.
