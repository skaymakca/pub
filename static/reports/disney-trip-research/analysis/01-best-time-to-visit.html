Tiered Recommendations

Tier 1: Best Overall (Highly Recommended)

Option A: April Vacation 2026 — Extended Start

Dates: Thu Apr 16 – Fri Apr 24 (fly Wed Apr 15 evening or Thu morning) - School days missed: 2 (Thu-Fri before break) - Why: Spring break crowds are largely gone by mid-April. Gorgeous 83°F weather. EPCOT Flower & Garden Festival in full swing. Cheerleading Worlds don't start until Apr 24 so you'd be leaving as they arrive. 9 full days at Disney is plenty for all 4 parks + pool days. - Cost level: Moderate (tickets ~$165/day, resort in Moderate Season) - Risks: If Easter falls differently some years, crowds may linger. 2026 Easter is Apr 5, so by Apr 16 it's well past.

Option B: Late January 2026 "Long Weekend Plus"

Dates: Thu Jan 22 – Mon Jan 26 (or Tue Jan 27) - School days missed: 2 (Thu-Fri) - Why: Jan 20-30 is one of the lowest crowd periods of the entire year. Value Season hotel pricing. Festival of the Arts at EPCOT is wonderful (and free with admission). Tickets ~$145/day. Cool, pleasant weather (71°F days, 50°F nights — bring layers). - Cost level: LOW — cheapest option that still has great weather - Risks: Only 4-5 park days (tight for all 4 parks + rest). Possible ride refurbishments in January. Cool evenings may not suit pool time.

Tier 2: Strong Options

Option C: Early November 2026

Dates: Sat Oct 31 – Fri Nov 6 - School days missed: 5 (Mon-Fri) - Why: Near-perfect weather (78°F). EPCOT Food & Wine Festival — amazing for foodie parents. Mickey's Not-So-Scary Halloween Party on Oct 31 would be unforgettable for the kids. Low-moderate crowds. - Cost level: Moderate-High on resort, moderate on tickets - Risks: 5 school days is a lot to miss. Fall resort pricing isn't discounted.

Option D: April Vacation 2026 — Straight Break

Dates: Sat Apr 18 – Sat Apr 25 - School days missed: 0 - Why: No school missed. Good (not great) crowds — tail end of spring break season. Beautiful weather. Flower & Garden Festival. - Cost level: Moderate - Risks: Apr 24 Cheerleading Worlds could spike Friday crowds. Book early as this is a popular NE school break week.

Tier 3: Budget Picks (Trade-offs Required)

Option E: Early September 2026

Dates: Tue Sep 8 – Sun Sep 13 - School days missed: 5 (first week of school) - Why: Absolute rock-bottom pricing on everything. Lowest crowds of the year (2-3). Up to 30% off resort. Free kids' dining. EPCOT Food & Wine Festival running. - Cost level: LOWEST possible - Risks: Brutal heat (90°F+ with extreme humidity, heat index 105°F+). Daily thunderstorms. Kids miss first week of school which is socially important. Hurricane season.

Option F: February Vacation 2026 (On-Break)

Dates: Sat Feb 14 – Sat Feb 21 - School days missed: 0 - Why: No school missed. Festival of the Arts. Pleasant weather (73°F). - Cost level: HIGH — February is the most expensive ticket month, and Presidents Day week adds resort demand - Risks: Highest ticket prices of the year. Elevated crowds from Presidents Day. Many NE school districts share this break week.

Our Recommendation

For this family: Option A (April extended) or Option D (April straight).

Rationale: - Wife wants pool time → need warm weather (rules out January) - Kids age 7 & 10 → can handle full park days, want ride variety - Foodies → Flower & Garden Festival has excellent food booths - Budget-conscious but willing to spend on quality → moderate pricing is fine, no need to chase September's rock-bottom - 83°F in April is perfect — warm enough for pools, not so hot that park days are miserable - Missing just 2 school days (Option A) gets you noticeably lower crowds than the straight break week

If pulling the kids out for 2 days feels fine, go with Option A. If not, Option D is still very solid.

Second choice: Option C (Early November) if you're willing to miss 5 school days. The Food & Wine Festival + Halloween combo would be incredible for a foodie family, and the weather is arguably the best of the year.
