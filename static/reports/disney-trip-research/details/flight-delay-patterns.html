  • Worst month for delays: July (summer thunderstorms + high volume)
  • Best month for delays: November (pre-holiday lull)
  • Winter storms: Jan-Feb see the highest cancellation rates (6-15% on BOS-MCO route). Major nor'easters can ground 500-1000 flights in a single event.

4. Delay Patterns by Time of Day

BOS Airport Delay Distribution

Time Window Delay Risk Notes
5:00 AM - 8:00 AM LOWEST Aircraft are fresh from overnight, no cascading delays yet
8:00 AM - 11:59 AM LOW Still reliable; most popular BOS-MCO departure window
12:00 PM - 4:59 PM MODERATE Delays begin accumulating; MCO thunderstorm window starts
5:00 PM - 9:00 PM HIGHEST Peak delay period at BOS; cascading delays from earlier disruptions
After 9:00 PM HIGH Residual delays; risk of overnight cancellation if crew times out

BOS-specific finding: Peak delays occur 5 PM to 9 PM. Fewest delays 5 AM to noon.

MCO-specific factor: Orlando thunderstorms typically fire between 2 PM and 6 PM local time during summer months (Jun-Sep). Flights arriving MCO in this window face higher weather delay risk in summer.

Recommendation: Book the earliest morning departure available. Flights before noon have significantly better on-time performance. Avoid evening departures, especially in winter.

What Drives BOS Winter Cancellations

  1. Nor'easters -- Major coastal storms bringing heavy snow, high winds, low visibility. Most common Jan-Mar.
  2. De-icing delays -- Even moderate snow/freezing rain adds 30-60 min to departure times.
  3. FAA ground stops -- When conditions deteriorate rapidly, FAA halts all departures.
  4. Crew/aircraft displacement -- One storm creates cascading cancellations for 1-2 days after.

Mitigation Strategies for Winter Travel

  • Book early morning flights -- If storms develop, AM flights are more likely to depart before conditions worsen.
  • Choose Delta -- Best winter reliability on this route (82% on-time even in Feb).
  • Avoid Friday evening -- Worst combination of high volume + potential weather.
  • Build in buffer days -- Don't fly the day of a critical event (park opening, cruise departure). Fly a day early.
  • Consider travel insurance -- Especially for Dec-Feb travel from BOS.
  • Monitor forecasts -- Airlines often issue travel waivers 24-48 hours before major storms, allowing free rebooking.

Summary: Best & Worst Times to Fly BOS-MCO

Best Conditions

  • Month: September or October (80-86% on-time, <2% cancellation)
  • Day: Tuesday, Wednesday, or Saturday
  • Time: Before 10 AM departure
  • Airline: Delta (81% on-time, lowest cancellation rate)

Worst Conditions

  • Month: February (65-71% on-time, 6-15% cancellation)
  • Day: Friday
  • Time: After 5 PM departure
  • Airline: JetBlue (68% on-time overall, 10.4% cancellation in Feb)

For a Disney Trip Specifically

If traveling in winter/spring break (the likely window): - Spring break (late Mar/Apr): Moderate risk. Weather improving but late storms possible. Book morning Delta flight. - February school vacation week: High risk. This is historically the worst month for BOS delays/cancellations. Build in a buffer day, book early AM, and consider trip insurance. - Summer (Jun-Aug): Moderate risk. BOS departures are good, but MCO arrivals face afternoon thunderstorm delays. Book flights arriving Orlando before 2 PM. - Fall (Sep-Nov): Best window for reliable flights. October is ideal.
