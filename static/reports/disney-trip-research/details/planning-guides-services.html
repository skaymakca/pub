1. Free Online Planning Resources

Overview

The Disney planning community is enormous. These are the most established, frequently recommended independent sites. All are free to access (some have optional paid products or display ads).

Touring Plans (touringplans.com)

URL: https://touringplans.com

What they offer: Data-driven Disney planning based on decades of crowd research by statisticians. Founded by Len Testa, co-author of The Unofficial Guide to Walt Disney World.

Feature Free Paid ($15.95-$24.99/yr)
Blog articles & news Yes Yes
Basic wait time info Yes Yes
Ticket price calculator Yes Yes
Dining reservation finder Yes Yes
Crowd Calendar (full) No Yes
Personalized touring plans No Yes
Predicted actual wait times (Lines app) Limited Yes
Room request tool (Room Finder) No Yes
Reservation Finder (dining alerts) No Yes
Menu search No Yes

Strengths: - Most data-driven resource available; crowd predictions based on statistical modeling - Wait time predictions are more accurate than Disney's posted times - Personalized touring plans optimize your park day minute-by-minute - Ticket calculator compares 160+ ticket configurations to find cheapest option - 45-day money-back guarantee on subscription

Weaknesses: - Best features are behind a paywall - Can feel overwhelming / overly analytical for casual planners - Crowd calendar is a prediction tool, not a guarantee

Best for: Analytical planners who want to optimize every hour; families who want to minimize wait times.

Walt Disney World Prep School (wdwprepschool.com)

URL: https://wdwprepschool.com

What they offer: Comprehensive free planning guides organized as a step-by-step "curriculum." Run by Shannon Albert. Strong podcast (Walt Disney World Prep To Go).

Strengths: - Excellent 6-step planning framework for beginners - Free crowd calendar - Very well-organized -- guides are structured logically for first-timers - Detailed Lightning Lane strategy guides with pricing caps - Active podcast with timely updates - Strong social media presence (Instagram, TikTok)

Weaknesses: - Ad-heavy site (standard for free content) - Some content can be repetitive across articles - Less data-driven than Touring Plans

Best for: First-time visitors who want a structured, step-by-step planning process.

Disney Tourist Blog (disneytouristblog.com)

URL: https://www.disneytouristblog.com

What they offer: In-depth planning guides, hotel reviews, restaurant reviews, park strategy, and photography. Run by Tom Bricker, a well-known Disney photographer and writer.

Strengths: - Exceptionally detailed and honest reviews (hotels, restaurants, attractions) - Beautiful photography throughout - Covers international Disney parks as well - Balanced, fair opinions with clear disclosure of bias - Highly regarded for reliability and credibility in the Disney community - Comprehensive trip planning guide updated for 2026

Weaknesses: - Articles can be very long (sometimes intimidatingly so for beginners) - Written primarily from an adult/couple perspective (less kid-focused) - No proprietary tools (crowd calendar, etc.)

Best for: Detail-oriented planners who want thorough, honest reviews; adults and couples.

AllEars.net

URL: https://allears.net

What they offer: One of the oldest Disney planning sites (est. 1996). News, reviews, planning guides, menus, and a major YouTube channel (AllEars TV).

Strengths: - Extremely comprehensive coverage -- every restaurant menu, attraction detail, resort room - AllEars TV YouTube channel is one of the most popular Disney channels - Free weekly newsletter with updates - Strong historical archive of Disney information - Quick to report on breaking Disney news and changes

Weaknesses: - Site design can feel dated / cluttered - Volume of content can be overwhelming - Less opinionated / more encyclopedic (sometimes you just want someone to tell you what to do)

Best for: Planners who want encyclopedic reference information and enjoy video content.

Disney Food Blog (disneyfoodblog.com)

URL: https://www.disneyfoodblog.com

What they offer: Comprehensive Disney dining coverage. Run by AJ Wolfe. Also offers paid Disney Food Blog Guides (e-books).

Weaknesses: - Dining focus may not suit those less interested in food - Paid guides overlap somewhat with free content - Heavy on sponsored content / affiliate links

Best for: Foodies planning Disney dining. Essential for your family given your foodie interests.

MouseSavers (mousesavers.com)

URL: https://www.mousesavers.com

What they offer: Aggregator of Disney discounts, deals, and coupon codes. Running since 2001. Free newsletter with exclusive Undercover Tourist deals.

Strengths: - Single best source for finding current Disney discounts - Monthly newsletter with exclusive ticket discounts (via Undercover Tourist partnership) - Covers hotel discounts, ticket deals, vacation package promos, and area hotel rates - Negotiated preferred hotel rates for off-site properties - No fluff -- purely deal-focused

Weaknesses: - Not a planning guide -- purely discount/deal focused - Site design is basic/utilitarian - Doesn't help with strategy or itineraries

Best for: Budget-conscious planners looking for every possible discount. Everyone should subscribe to the newsletter.

MouseHacking (mousehacking.com)

URL: https://www.mousehacking.com

What they offer: Comprehensive planning guides with detailed budget breakdowns. Includes park-specific one-day itineraries.

Strengths: - Excellent budget breakdown (estimates $5,187-$12,110 for family of four in 2026) - Park-specific itineraries (one-day plans for each park) - Lightning Lane strategy guides - "Super Simple" guide for people who don't want to read 50 articles - Clean, well-organized site

Weaknesses: - Smaller operation, less frequently updated than larger sites - Less community engagement - Fewer reviews (restaurants, hotels)

Best for: Budget-conscious planners who want concrete cost estimates and simple itineraries.

EasyWDW / yourfirstvisit.net

URL: https://yourfirstvisit.net (EasyWDW blog shut down July 2022; guidebook continues)

What they offer: Exact, prescriptive instructions for first-time Disney World visitors. Created by Dave Shute, co-author of The easy Guide to Your Walt Disney World Visit.

Strengths: - Tells you exactly what to do, when, and where -- no ambiguity - Week-by-week itineraries with daily guidance - Detailed resort room diagrams and reviews - Endorsed by former Walt Disney World EVP of Operations Lee Cockerell - Guidebook updated regularly with PDF updates sent to purchasers

Weaknesses: - Blog (easyWDW) shut down in 2022; yourfirstvisit.net continues but updates less frequently - Very prescriptive style may not suit everyone - Less current than actively maintained blogs

Best for: First-time visitors who want to be told exactly what to do.

3. Free Authorized Disney Travel Agents

How "Free" Travel Agents Work

Disney pays commissions to Authorized Disney Vacation Planners when they book Disney resort stays, tickets, and dining packages. You pay the exact same price as booking directly with Disney -- sometimes less, because agents can apply discounts and monitor for price drops. There is no fee to you.

What they do: - Book your resort, tickets, and dining package at the same price as Disney direct - Monitor for new promotions and automatically rebook at lower prices - Make dining reservations (often at the 60-day mark when things open) - Provide planning advice and itinerary suggestions - Handle all phone calls with Disney on your behalf

EarMarked Designation Levels (Disney's rating system): - Diamond -- highest level; indicates top sales volume, training compliance, and customer satisfaction - Platinum -- second tier - Gold -- third tier

The Vacationeer

URL: https://vacationeer.com EarMarked Status: Diamond Cost: Free

Services: - Dedicated personal agent (no call centers) - Proprietary "Magical Planning" system -- structured roadmap ensuring nothing is missed - Continuous price monitoring with automatic re-booking at lower prices - Dining reservations made on your behalf - Up to $1,000 onboard credit for Disney Cruise bookings - Up to $1,500 savings on Adventures by Disney

Reviews: 1,700+ verified 5-star reviews on Google. Inc. 5000 recognized. Ranked #1 Disney agency by Magic Guides for 2026. ~150-agent elite team.

Best for: Families who want a highly structured, proven planning process with a dedicated agent.

MEI-Travel / Mouse Fan Travel

URL: https://mousefantravel.com / https://mei-travel.com EarMarked Status: Authorized Disney Vacation Planner Cost: Free

Services: - 20+ years of Disney-specific vacation planning experience - All agents licensed and insured - Covers Walt Disney World, Disneyland, Disney Cruise Line, Adventures by Disney - Recommended by AllEars.net and multiple Disney fan sites/blogs

Reviews: Highly recommended across the Disney media and fan community. Strong reputation for agent expertise and onboarding quality.

Best for: Families who value experienced, well-trained agents with deep Disney knowledge.

Small World Vacations

URL: https://www.smallworldvacations.com EarMarked Status: Diamond (highest level) Cost: Free

Services: - 25+ years in business - Quotation system matches you with the best agent for your needs - Monitor new promotions and rebook at better prices - A+ rating from Better Business Bureau - Payments go directly to Disney (never held by the agency)

Reviews: Consistently positive on DISboards, TripAdvisor, and Touring Plans forums. Known for responsive, knowledgeable agents.

Best for: Families who want a long-established, highly credentialed agency with a strong track record.

MickeyTravels

URL: https://mickeytravels.com EarMarked Status: Diamond (3rd consecutive year, 2023-2025) Cost: Free

Services: - All agents are Disney College of Knowledge graduates - 14+ years of Disney-only vacation planning - Personalized planning services - Associated with MickeyBlog.com for news and tips

Reviews: Strong testimonials on their site. Diamond status for three consecutive years demonstrates consistent quality.

Best for: Families who want agents exclusively focused on Disney with strong training credentials.

Dreams Unlimited Travel

URL: https://www.dreamsunlimitedtravel.com EarMarked Status: Authorized Disney Vacation Planner Cost: Free

Services: - Assigned personal Vacation Consultant throughout planning - Constant discount monitoring between booking and departure - Shipboard credits + gift baskets for Disney Cruise bookings - Agents respond within 24 hours, including weekends - BBB accredited

Reviews: Positive across DISboards (which Dreams Unlimited is affiliated with) and TripAdvisor. Praised for responsive email-based communication.

Best for: Families who prefer email-based communication and want Disney Cruise extras.

Ears of Experience

URL: https://www.earsofexperience.com EarMarked Status: Platinum Cost: Free

Services: - Founded 2009; large virtual agent network - All agents are College of Disney Knowledge graduates - Extensive in-house training program - Makes the most military reservations with Disney annually

Reviews: Mixed. Positive reviews praise responsive agents. Some critics note that prices are identical to Disney direct (which is how all free agents work -- this isn't unique to them).

Best for: Military families (strongest military booking record); families wanting a large agent network.

4. Paid Concierge / VIP Planning Services

Disney's Official VIP Services

Disney Private VIP Tours

URL: https://disneyworld.disney.go.com/events-tours/private-vip-tours/ Price: $425-$750/hour, 7-hour minimum ($2,975-$5,250+ per day)

Feature Details
Group size Up to 10 guests
What's included Private guide, private vehicle, back-entrance park access, special ride access lines
What's NOT included Park admission (must buy separately), food, tips
Tipping 10-15% customary
Availability Varies by season; book well in advance

Disney Signature Services

URL: https://disneyworld.disney.go.com/guest-services/signature-services/ Price: Complimentary for eligible guests Eligibility: Must have a Private VIP Tour booked OR a confirmed reservation for Club-Level rooms, Suites, Grand Villas, select premium room types

Feature Details
Advance Dining Reservations Personalized assistance
VIP tour booking Priority access
Cabana rentals Assistance booking
Fireworks cruises Assistance booking
Special dietary needs Menu item info and allergy notation

Third-Party Paid Planning Services

These are independent companies that charge a fee for planning expertise beyond what free agents provide.

Service Price Range What They Offer Beyond Free Agents
Walt Disney World Travel Guys (wdwtravelguys.com) ~$50/guest Concierge-level itinerary planning, personalized daily schedules
Magical Travel (magicaltravel.com) $150-$300/trip Tiered packages: Basic ($150, up to 4 nights, 60 min phone consult) to Fairytale Plus ($300, up to 10 people, 7 nights)
Get Away Today (getawaytoday.com) Free concierge + discount tickets Best price guarantee; concierge planning included with ticket/package purchase
My VIP Tour (myviptour.com) ~$450+/hour (6-hr min) In-park private touring with non-Disney guides

What paid services offer that free agents don't: - Detailed minute-by-minute daily itineraries - In-park touring guides (third-party, not Disney) - Extended phone consultations - Personalized packing lists, countdown calendars, etc.

Important caveat: Some third-party in-park touring services may violate Disney's property rules regarding unauthorized commercial activities. Stick with Disney's official VIP Tours for in-park guide services.

Phone: (407) 939-5277 (407-Walt Disney World-PARK) Price: Free

Cast Members can help book resorts, tickets, dining, and answer general planning questions. Available daily.

Verdict: Useful for specific booking questions but not a substitute for a free travel agent who will proactively monitor prices and plan strategically.

7. Recommendations for This Family

Based on your profile -- two parents, kids aged 7 and 10, foodies, traveling from Newton MA:

Tier 1: Essential (Do These)

Action Cost Why
Book through a free travel agent (The Vacationeer or Small World Vacations) $0 Zero downside. Same price as Disney direct, but someone monitors for discounts, makes dining reservations at the 60-day mark, and handles all logistics. Both are Diamond EarMarked.
Subscribe to Touring Plans ~$20/yr The crowd calendar helps pick your dates, the personalized touring plans save hours of waiting, and the Lines app gives real-time edge in the parks. Best $20 you'll spend.
Download My Disney Experience app $0 Non-negotiable. You need this for Lightning Lane, mobile ordering, wait times, and park maps.
Subscribe to MouseSavers newsletter $0 Monthly exclusive ticket discounts via Undercover Tourist. Takes 30 seconds.

Tier 2: Highly Recommended (For Your Family Specifically)

Action Cost Why
Buy the Disney Food Blog Guide to Walt Disney World Dining ~$20 You're foodies. This is the definitive Disney dining reference. Use it to plan dining reservations and discover hidden gems.
Read Walt Disney World Prep School's planning guide $0 Best structured intro for first-timers. Follow their 6-step process.
Read Disney Tourist Blog's hotel and restaurant reviews $0 The most honest, detailed reviews available. Essential for choosing your resort.
Check Undercover Tourist for tickets Savings of ~4% Could save $40-80 on family tickets. Compare to Disney direct.

Tier 3: Nice to Have

Action Cost Why
Browse AllEars.net / AllEars TV $0 Good for visual learners; YouTube videos help you "preview" parks and restaurants.
Use MouseHacking budget calculator $0 Sanity-check your budget against their family-of-four estimates.
Join DISboards $0 Ask specific questions to the community (e.g., "best pool for kids at Polynesian?").

What to Skip

Option Why Skip It
Paid planning concierge ($50-$300) Free travel agents provide 95% of the same service. Not worth it at your trip scale.
Disney VIP Tour ($3,000-$5,000+/day) Amazing but massive cost. Your kids are 7 and 10 -- they'll love the parks regardless. Consider this for a future "once in a lifetime" anniversary trip without kids.
Disney Club Level / Signature Services Good perk if you're already booking a Deluxe resort suite, but don't upgrade just for the planning service.

Recommended Planning Timeline

  1. Now: Subscribe to MouseSavers newsletter and Touring Plans. Read Walt Disney World Prep School's 6-step guide.
  2. Pick dates: Use Touring Plans crowd calendar + your school calendar analysis.
  3. Book agent: Contact The Vacationeer or Small World Vacations. They'll handle resort + tickets + packages.
  4. 60 days before: Agent makes dining reservations. Use Disney Food Blog Guide to prioritize restaurants.
  5. 30 days before: Download My Disney Experience. Link tickets. Set up MagicBand+.
  6. 7 days before: Use Touring Plans to build personalized park-day itineraries.
  7. In the parks: Use Lines app (Touring Plans) + My Disney Experience for real-time decisions.
